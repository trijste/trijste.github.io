Dear The Voice

What a windy way to wear the world, warrior. Wouldn't watering wayward worries wrap wounds with winter weather? Walter wonders...

I shaved my face and look like a baby. I miss the ugly pubic hair that grew from my chin--luckily, it will grow back in time. I went to this high school valentine dance party at the 'Centre Acadien' tonight. It was just me and three other weirdos from my group. I dressed up spiffy with a jacket and scarf and boogied like a fresh master while young girls and guys oogled on. Whack music but flicking great, and we are sure to be the talk of the town come Monday. I danced so hard I popped a button off my shirt! Wish you were here to hug me and laugh loudly, but am glad you are where you are doing what you're doing--which sounds so flooking incredible! Will I be able to see mister famous when I get back, or will you be off in cloud city making dreams? I am actually seriously proud and excited. It just sounds like a super string of moment things lining up to create hype heaven, hero. Fuck Marla city! Does she know yet, and are you going to tell her what a shitty job she has been and always will be doing? Eugene Levy! I went to Italy with that guy's son! I forget his name, but he looked a lot like him. Keep me posted on all the happenings in all the departments. I am getting a little frustrated in my own department. Not much on the scene, but it all gets prettier as the days drift by. I wake up like a rock every morning. I refuse to beat myself up about it. It wouldn't do me any good (catch all the inuendo?). I am falling back on a fantasy I have of hiking into the woods with one of the girls to camp out and drink mickeys of fireball and gordon's gin on my 48 hours off. 'We'll be a lot warmer if we share a shleeping bag.' HahaHA! I love you. I have a phone card so I'll try to call you before Tuesday to say hello. NEW YORK CITY. I want to go with you someday, so get to know the town while you're there.
I am definately getting experience, but my job is pretty moopsed up. The best part is working with the clients, who are very very affectionate and hilarious; the worst part is the work I have to do, which will be painting lobster traps the colours of the Acadian flag--two coats each. The highlights are that I'm learning how to knit socks, which is serious in my books. Socks lead to gloves and sleeves of sweaters that I will learn to knit in time. I'm getting pretty good at it, and will hopefully learn to crochet at some point. I also just take my time and listen to the 720 AM oldies station that crackles in from PEI. It's pretty relaxed actually, so I shouldn't shit all over it. Next week I am one of the two weekly house managers, who cook three meals a day for the twelve people in the house and clean and shop and do dishes. I'll get through it, and when I do I'll be starting half days at the museum doing that film editing work.

Happy Birthday --> I'd send you a post card if I knew your address.

I drew a strange picture I'll show sometime. The art up here travels strangley through the pens.

PEACE - Tristan
