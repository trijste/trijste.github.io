To Aid An_ Cage

2005-08-05 - 1:00 p.m.

application
I went out with Stacey last night to visit a friend of hers in Oakville. Amanda is part Metis, and just got back from a two month canoe trip across the northern regions of Ontario, Manitoba and Saskatchewan. If I am part Metis, which I doubt I am, I would be able to do a six month trip from one of the corners of North America. I must begin to look into a possible tie to the Metis tribe.
Kirsten can't speak and has a fever.
I came into the OCAD office to find out about enrolling, and the guy told me I had to apply to become a student, then enroll in the non-student admission program. Even then the courses I want to take I would have to beg the staff to let me into and there is a chance they would say no. What do I do if I just want to take three or four courses over the winter/spring? I guess I have to look into another type of continuing education program somewhere else, moops! Time ticks on. I have lots of work doing, with part time cash from Liam's kitchen, a day at the ACC, and part time work at TEA. Work is great and all, but I really want to be producing art while I'm in the city, and without the structure of class time and projects, I worry about my own ability to churn them out. I fear that, like my crochet bag that sits unfinished, I will never properly give myself to a piece.
I am typing from the free computers in the OCAD office, which I wasn't sure existed. I should get off before I get kicked out. I am going to meet Carter for an audition read, then call Phriam to find out about a hard nights work.

PEACE - Tristan
